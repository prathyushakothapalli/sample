Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (born 5 July 1995)[5] is an Indian badminton player.[6] Considered one of India's most successful sportspersons, Sindhu has won medals at various tournaments such as the Olympics and on the BWF circuit, including a gold at the 2019 World Championships. She is the first and only Indian to become the badminton world champion and only the second individual athlete from India to win two consecutive medals at the Olympic Games.[7] She rose to a career-high world ranking of no. 2 in April 2017.[8] Sindhu broke into the top 20 of the BWF World Rankings in September 2012, at the age of 17.[9] She has won a total of five medals at the BWF World Championships and is only the second woman after China's Zhang Ning ever to win five or more singles medals in the competition. She represented India at the 2016 Summer Olympics (Rio), where she became the first Indian badminton player to reach the Olympic final. She won the silver medal after losing out to Spain's Carolina Marín.[10] She made her second consecutive Olympic appearance at the 2020 Summer Olympics (Tokyo) and won a bronze medal, becoming the first-ever Indian woman to win two Olympic medals.[11][12] Sindhu won her first superseries title at the 2016 China Open and followed it up with four more finals in 2017, winning the titles in South Korea and India. She also won the 2018 BWF World Tour Finals and remains the only Indian player to win a season finale title. In addition to that, she is the reigning Commonwealth Games champion and has won three consecutive singles medals at the Commonwealth Games, a silver medal at the Asian Games, and two bronze medals at the Uber Cup.

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu was born and brought up in Hyderabad, India to P. V. Ramana and P. Vijaya.[5][18] Ramana's ancestors belong to Eluru district of Andhra Pradesh and later moved to Guntur.[19][20] Ramana was an employee of the Indian Railways and was born in Nirmal. Vijaya hails from Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh.[19][21] Sindhu and her family regularly offer prayers to their family deity in Ratnalammakunta village of Eluru district.[22][23][24] Both her parents have been national level volleyball players. Her father, Ramana and was a member of the Indian volleyball team that won the bronze medal in the 1986 Seoul Asian Games, received the Arjuna Award in 2000 for his contribution to the sport.[25] Sindhu lives in Hyderabad. She was educated at Auxilium High School[26] and at St. Ann's College for Women, Hyderabad.[27] Though her parents played professional volleyball, she chose badminton over it because she drew inspiration from the success of Pullela Gopichand, the 2001 All England Open Badminton Champion.[28] She eventually started playing badminton from the age of eight.[25] She first learned the basics of the sport with the guidance of Mehboob Ali at the badminton courts of the Indian Railway Institute of Signal Engineering and Telecommunications in Secunderabad.
